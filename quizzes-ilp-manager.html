Inclusive Leader Profile

Manager Edition

The Inclusive Leader Profile (ILP) is based on 20 years of information gathered in one-on-one interviews, as well as focus groups and thousands of diversity training sessions.

The ILP© is about the subtle things people do, or don't do, that lead those who are diverse or to feel isolated, powerless, or in any way "less valued."

The ILP© is based on the belief that most people don't intend to discriminate, judge, or isolate others. It often happens without intent, but the impact can be devastating to individuals and an organization. Taking this "quiz" will give you an opportunity to evaluate your behaviors and to offer you awareness of how to modify them in order to be more respectful of all people.

A note about terminology

Culturally Diverse Individual:

Wherever the term culturally diverse individual is used on this page, it refers to people who are different from you or have a different culture from yours.



Culture:

Culture is defined as "patterns of thought (values, believes, and attitudes) and behavior shared within a group of people." We take our culture for granted and seldom think about it.

When we share a common culture with a group, we tend to go about our lives in similar ways. We share similar beliefs about values, sickness, medicine, life, death, family, work, decision-making, time, formality, and a host of other things.

In today's multicultural world, people are continually interacting with little awareness of how cultures may collide.

 

How often do I, as a manager...

Think about the impact of my comments and actions before I speak or act?

 
 

Check out reality before repeating or believing rumors or assumptions about anyone?

 
 

Let employees know what I expect of them in order to maintain a respectful work environment?

 
 

Confront employees who are acting inappropriately or speaking offensively?

 
 

Get to know employees from different cultures and backgrounds in order to learn what they need to succeed?

 
 

Refuse to participate in derogatory jokes and rumors about any individual or group, even behind closed doors?

 
 

Avoid using language that reinforces negative stereotypes (e.g., "That's just the way they are")?

 
 

Accommodate the work/life challenges employees face without judgment or animosity?

 
 

Identify barriers that keep individuals from performing to their full potential and work to remove them?

 
 

Recognize when my biases and prejudices are influencing my behaviors?

 
 

Challenge the belief that everyone has to be similar to be successful?

 
 

Include individuals from different cultures in formal or informal networks and social events?

 
 

Learn about other cultures and honor their holidays and events?

 
 

Remain flexible to accommodate different work styles?

 
 

Consider diversity when selecting team members?

 
 

Plan social events in which all people are able to participate (considering scheduling, dietary restrictions, religious traditions, etc.)?

 
 

Encourage individuals of diverse backgrounds to talk about their issues and concerns?

 
 

Solicit input from employees who may have perspectives different from mine?

 
 

Take responsibility for orienting new employees so they feel welcomed and accepted?

 
 

Provide important and challenging work assignments to employees of diverse cultures and backgrounds in accordance with their skills and talents?

 
 

Listen to ideas from employees, including those of diverse backgrounds, and give them credit, both individually and in group meetings?

 
 

Provide straightforward and timely feedback to all employees, including those of diverse cultures and backgrounds?

 
 

Disregard physical characteristics when determining abilities or job competencies?

 
 

Match my words about diversity and inclusion with my actions and behaviors?

 
 

Conduct career development discussions with all employees?

 
 

Hire individuals based on objective criteria rather than my personal preferences, conveniences, or traditions?

 
 

Keep culturally diverse individuals in the formal and informal information loops?

 
 

Provide all employees with ongoing development opportunities, including those of diverse cultures and backgrounds?

 
 

Resolve conflict and disagreement in a culturally sensitive manner?

 
 

Provide equal opportunities for all individuals to achieve career advancement?

 
 

Provide formal and informal mentoring to help individuals of diverse backgrounds learn how we do things?

 
 

Find out the real reasons why individuals leave the organization and work to eliminate these reasons for others?

 
 

Remain open to receiving coaching and feedback from my employees?

 
 

Ask questions when I am unsure about what to do in a situation with a person who is different from me?

 
 

Recognize that behaviors and words may have different meanings across cultures?

 
 

Work to change organizational policies and procedures that lead to the exclusion of anyone?
