YOUR 30-DAY GUIDE

Onboarding at Turner is a process that happens over an extended period of time. It lays the groundwork for new employees to become productive and successful team members for the long haul. Employees who experience an effective onboarding program are 58% more likely to be with the organization after three years. Research suggests that managers have the number one impact on employees' overall onboarding experience.
DAY 1 Obtain your badge.

Register for parking (if available at your location).

Complete your I-9 documentation.

Set up your network access. Connect with your manager.
WITHIN 7 DAYS Set up your workstation.

Meet with your manager to set initial expectations, learn about the team, etc.

Go on a CNN Center tour or arrange for a tour of your work location.

WITHIN 30 DAYS Enroll in your health & wellness benefits (if applicable).

Complete any required training.

Establish a rhythm of one-on-one meetings with your manager(s) and direct report(s).

Build your network.

4 KEY CONVERSATIONS YOU SHOULD HAVE
  • FIT
  • FOCUS
  • FRIENDS
  • FUTURE
PEOPLE LEADERS
With your manager, talk about:
What kind of culture do I want to create or reinforce on my team? What norms and values do I want to instill?
How does my team interact with my immediate and wider departments?
How will we interact?
How regularly will we check in?
How do our styles differ and what are the implications for how we'll interact?
With your team, talk about:
As you build each relationship, discuss each person's preferences and needs.
How often and how do you want to be updated? How will you cascade information?
As a team, discuss expectations and how you can work together effectively.
INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTORS:
With your manager, talk about:
What is the culture on the team? What are the norms and values that you expect?
How does the team interact with our immediate and wider departments?
How will we interact? How regularly will we check in?
How do our styles differ and what are the implications for how we'll interact?
PEOPLE LEADERS
With your manager, talk about:
What information do I need to know about my leaders' expectations for me and my team?
How does my team fit into the department's goals?
What results do you expect me to achieve within the net few weeks?
What does success look like to you? How will performance be measured?
With your team, talk about:
Clearly describe and discuss goals and priorities for your team. What does success look like? How will performance be measured?
INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTORS:
With your manager, talk about:
What information do I need to know about my leader's expectations of me?
How does my team fit into my department's goals?
What results do you expect me to achieve within the next few weeks?
What does success look like to you? How will performance be measured?
PEOPLE LEADERS
With your manager, talk about:
Who are the key stakeholders I need to reach out to immediately?
Which stakeholders do I need to build relationships with as my support network?
What are the department's priorities?
What do I need to know about the structure and capabilities of my team?
Discuss performance (successful behaviors and development areas) for each person on your team.
With your team, talk about:
Ask what the team know about the department's goals and priorities?
INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTORS:
With your manager, talk about:
Who are the key stakeholders I need to reach out to immediately?
Which stakeholders do I need to build relationships with as my support network?
What are my organization's priorities?
PEOPLE LEADERS
With your manager, talk about:
What challenges might the business face within the next 12-18 months? Beyond? What is the impact to the services my team provides?
What unexplored opportunities should I consider? What would need to happen to realize them?
How can I continue to develop my skills? What tools and resources are available?
With your team, talk about:
What are their career goals and aspirations?
What development plans do they currently have and how can you support them?
INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTORS:
With your manager, talk about:
What challenges might the business face within the next 12-18 months? Beyond? What is the impact to the services I provide?
What unexplored opportunities should I propose? What would need to happen to realize them?
How can I continue to develop my skills? What tools and resources are available?
Create an individual development plan.
