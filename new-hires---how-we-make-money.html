How We Make Money


Revenue

Dual Revenue Streams
Although each one of our networks has unique content and programming, they make money the same way — from a dual revenue stream powered by advertising sales and revenue generated by content licensing fees.



Content Sales
We sell our content to cable operators, satellite providers, and telcos (e.g., AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, etc.).

In turn, they package our products and sell it as a subscription offering to their customers.

Turner also licenses branded content from our networks and brands on platforms such as smart phones, tablets, electronic storefronts, and video-on-demand services.



Advertising Sales
In addition to generating license fees, the distribution of content creates the audience foundation for Turner's ad sales business. Advertisers and ad agencies want their commercials seen by a particular viewing audience. The purchase price of the advertising is based on three factors:

  1. Capacity
    The number of hours available to sell.
  2. Demos
    Advertisers use demographics to determine the type of audience viewing particular content.
  3. Ratings
    These tell advertisers how many people are watching the network.
