How Well Do You Handle Uncertainty?

So, there's a lot going on. Our industry is changing, our business is changing, even we could be changing (we heard frosted tips are making a comeback). Each day brings with it some uncertainty and how we react to that ambiguity says a lot about us, according to researchers. Our ability to react to uncertainty has been connected to our openness to other people, ability to think creatively, and make smart decisions under pressure.

The notion above is what researcher Arie Kruglanski calls "need for cognitive closure." Kruglanski and psychologist Donna Webster developed the "need for closure scale" to measure this. In a nutshell, the thought is that those that need closure prefer black-and-white and feel uncomfortable when left hanging. On the opposite end, those who have lower needs for closure seem to have an easier time seeing "the gray" and can appreciate ambiguity.

You can take this brief assessment developed by psychologists Arne Roets and Alain Van Hiel — they adapted it from Webster and Kruglanski's longer version.

  1. I don't like situations that are uncertain.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  2. I dislike questions that could be answered in different ways.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  3. I feel uncomfortable when I don't understand the reason why an event occurred in my life.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  4. When I have made a decision, I feel relieved.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  5. I dislike it when a person's statement could mean many different things.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  6. I do not usually consult many different opinions before forming my own views.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  7. I find a well-ordered life with regular hours suits my temperament.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  8. I don't like to go into a situation without knowing what I can expect from it.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  9. I don't like to be with people who are capable of unexpected actions.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  10. I enjoy having a clear and structured mode of life.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  11. I feel irritated when one person disagrees with what everyone else in the group believes.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  12. I would quickly become impatient and irritated if I could not find a solution to a problem immediately.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  13. When I am confronted with a problem, I'm dying to reach a solution very quickly.

  14. I find that establishing a consistent routine enables me to enjoy life more.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me

  15. I dislike unpredictable situations.

    Not at all true of me   Very true of me
