So, what can you do?

 

If you find yourself in the NAIVE stage:

  • Start learning about others today
  • Find out what you are saying and doing that is offensive — then stop
  • Identify people that can serve as cultural guides and educators (e.g., people on your team, at Turner, in your community, etc.) — spend time with them and be open to their suggestions
  • Find other resources for learning by investigating other cultures through reading or attending events
 

If you find yourself in the PERPETUATOR stage:

  • Admit what you have been doing and stop it immediately
  • Consider what causes you to act or react in negative ways
  • Seek additional coaching or education from others
  • Apologize to those who have been negatively impacted by your behaviors
  • Ask others to call you on your actions when you slip into perpetuator behaviors.
 

If you find yourself in the AVOIDER stage:

  • Intervene in culturally appropriate ways when others do something inappropriate in the workplace
  • Be honest with your coworkers and provide them with feedback when you observe inappropriate behaviors
  • Have courage; practice and prepare for what you will say and do
 

If you find yourself in the FIGHTER stage:

Here's the thing with fighters: they typically feel so strongly about stopping injustice and are so diligent in spotting bias that they attack others instead of enlighten and educate. Obviously, this doesn't do much to create a positive environment. So...

  • Keep your passion, but educate yourself and develop the best skills that you can
  • Get some coaching from an inclusive leader on your approach
 

And if you're an INCLUSIVE LEADER:

  • Continue to speak out
  • Approach people with respect; speak to their behaviors in a clear, non-judgmental manner
  • Get coaching from other inclusive leaders as needed
  • Acknowledge positive intent of others
  • Coach others to deliver their message differently so that people will be more open to it
